Lando Norris biography

Norris’ interest in motorcycle racing shifted to four wheels after his father took him and his brother to watch a round of the Super 1 National Kart Championships at his local kart track.

He began racing at the age of seven, when he won pole position in his first national event. Norris won the CIK FIA European Championship, the International Super Cup, and the WSK Euro Series in KF-Junior karting in 2013.

The following year, with Ricky Flynn Motorsport, he won the CIK-FIA KF World Championship, making him the youngest karting world champion.

Norris debuted in the Ginetta Junior Championship, a support series to the British Touring Car Championship, in 2014. He finished third in the championship after winning four races and taking home the Rookie Cup.

Norris signed a contract with Carlin Motorsport for the 2015 MSA Formula Championship. Norris won the championship with eight wins, ten pole positions, and fourteen total podiums, finishing ahead of Ricky Collard and Colton Herta.

He also raced with Mücke Motorsport in the ADAC and Italian Formula 4 championships, claiming six podiums from eight starts in the former.

Lando Norris early life

Later, his family relocated to Glastonbury to allow him to attend day school and pursue his racing career, citing Valentino Rossi as an inspiration. He lived in Woking near the McLaren team headquarters at first, but moved to Monaco in 2022.
