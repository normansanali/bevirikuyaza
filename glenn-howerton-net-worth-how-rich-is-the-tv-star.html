Glenn Howerton Early Life

Glenn Franklin Howerton Jr. was born on April 13, 1976, to Janice and Glenn Franklin Howerton. His father used to be a fighter pilot. Howerton was born in Japan but spent his childhood in London, Alabama, and South Korea.

Glenn Howerton went to Jefferson Davis High School in Montgomery, Alabama. After graduating, he went on to study for two years at the New World School of the Arts in Miami, Florida.

Glenn Howerton was a member of the Juilliard School’s Drama Division’s Group 29 from 1996 to 2000. Glenn earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree.

Glenn Howerton Net Worth & Career

Glenn Howerton’s first starring role was as Corey Howard in That ’80s Show, which aired in 2000. The following year, he appeared as Dr. Nick Cooper on ER as a guest star. He appeared in Must Love Dogs and Serenity in 2005.

In 2006, he had minor roles in Crank and Two Weeks. He also appeared in The Strangers and Happy Wednesday two years later. He appeared in both Crank: High Voltage and American Dad! in 2009.

Between 2009 and 2010, Howerton played a variety of characters in Glenn Martin, DDS. From 2009 to 2013, he portrayed Ernie and voiced several other characters in The Cleveland Show.

Other roles in the 2010s include Gary in Everything Must Go, Will in Coffee Town, Donald De Vore in Vietnam in HD, Don Chump in Fargo, Seth Buckley in House of Lies, and Jack of A.P. Bio.

Glenn Howerton has also provided voices for characters such as Unsupervised, Family Guy, and Tip Tank.
