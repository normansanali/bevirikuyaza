Biography of Woody Harrelson

The 23rd of July 1961 saw the birth of Woodrow “Woody” Tracy Harrelson in Midland, Texas. The parents of Harrelson are Diane Lou and Charles Voyde. When little Harrelson was only seven years old, his father was found guilty of killing Federal Judge John H. Wood, Jr. and given a life sentence.

He graduated from Lebanon High School with a diploma. He worked as a woodcarver apprentice at Kings Island Amusement Park in addition to studying.

In 1985, Harrelson made his professional acting debut in the fourth season of the already well-known NBC sitcom “Cheers,” playing the bartender Woody Boyd. This marked the start of Harrelson’s acting career.

He made his big-screen debut while working on “Cheers” with the 1986 release of the football comedy “Wildcats,” in which he appeared in a cameo. After that, he appeared in a number of films, including “White Guys Can’t Jump,” “Money Train,” “LA Story,” and others.

His acting career got off to a wonderful start when he was cast in the movie “Indecent Proposal,” which also starred Demi Moore and Robert Redford. The movie was a huge hit and made about $265,000,000 in total at the box office.

He appeared in “No Country for Old Men” in 2007. The film’s excellent plot, fantastic performances, and some remarkable direction helped it become quite successful.

He subsequently appeared as a character in the slasher comedy “Zombieland,” and then he appeared in Roland Emmerich’s “2012.”

He appeared in the futuristic Western martial arts movie Bunraku from 2010 before moving on to the movie Friends with Benefits. He was just cast in the role of Haymitch Abernathy in the movie “The Hunger Games.”
