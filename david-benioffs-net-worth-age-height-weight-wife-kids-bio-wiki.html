Early Life

David Benioff was born to Barbara Benioff and Stephen Friedman. Stephen Friedman was the former head of Goldman Sachs. He was born into this Jewish family on 25th September 1970 in New York City. He had two elder siblings, Suzy and Caroline. David Benioff is also distantly related to the entrepreneur, Marc Benioff. His early childhood residence was in Manhattan, but then he moved with his family to 86th street, which was where he spent most of his life. When he was sixteen, the family moved to a place near the U.N. Headquarters.

David Benioff went to The Collegiate School. Then after graduating, he went to Dartmouth College. After college, he did several odd jobs, including as a bouncer of a club. He also got to be an English teacher at a school in Brooklyn. David Benioff also wanted to pursue a career in academia, and with this target, he went to Trinity College, Dublin. While at the college where he was studying Irish literature, David Benioff wrote a thesis on Samuel Becket. Soon after this, he went to get a job as a radio jockey because he had lost interest in academics. Later, he joined a creative writing programme at the University of California Irvine from where he received his master’s degree in the year 1999.

David Benioff got married to Amanda Peet in 2006. The couple now has three children, Frances Pen Friedman, Molly June Friedman and Henry Peet Friedman.

For instance, the screenplays for movies, like “Troy”, the psychological thriller “Stay” and “The Kite Runner” in 2007.
