John Yogi Hughes Celtic Wife, Family CELTIC Coach Passed Away After Cancer Related Illness

John Hughes was a former Celtic footballer much more favored by his nickname “Yogi.” His fans gave him the title based on his big appearance, which felt similar to the cartoon character Yogi Bear.

The former Scottish footballer was also a part of the Celtic team that won the European Cup in 1966-67 and was later named Lisbon Lions. While he could not contribute much in the finals because of an injury, his contribution to the team before the finals was undoubtedly essential.

Sadly, such a notable Celtic athlete breathe his last moments on August 1st, 2022. The news of his demise has shaken all his fans and family. Similarly, Many Rest In Peace posts and condolences are pouring in on Twitter and Facebook.

The Scottish footballer is said to have succumbed to an illness related to cancer. His name will now be counted among the immortals as long the Celtic Team exists.

CELTIC Star John “Yogi” Hughes’ Wife Theresa Was By His Side In The Last Moments

The Lisbon Lion member and Celtic legend had lived a fulfilling life and had no worries in his last moments. With his wife Theresa and his children by his side, John passed away after a short illness caused by his cancer.

John married Theresa after his first wife, Mary, died unexpectedly, leaving him behind with three sons and a daughter to care for. His children include Kevin, Martin, John, and his daughter Joanna.

After his marriage to Theresa, they had twin daughters, Victoria and Jessica, who are already adults and keen footballers under their father’s teachings.

John Yogi Huges Has A Huge Family With His Many Children And Six Grandchildren

Yogi left behind a big family who will always cherish the moments with him and keep him alive forever his their memories.

With five children and many grandchildren, John will be immortal in the hearts of his loved ones. In these moments of loss, his kids and their extended family have asked for privacy during this terribly difficult time, and they will reveal the funeral details later in due time.

In the meantime, his children are keeping the family together and are holding themselves from breaking down and drown in sorrow.

CELTIC Coach John Yogi Hughes Passed Away After Cancer Related Illness

Back in time, John Hughes was diagnosed with mouth cancer. In 2008, he fought for nearly five years while undergoing a rigorous chemotherapy regimen.

He endured lengthy hospital visits and dealt with numerous challenges throughout the program, including losing his teeth and hair.

John had multiple surgeries, including one to open up his narrowed throat so he could eat after receiving chemotherapy and radiation treatment.

Additionally, the Celtic legend described those five drawn-out years as a torment. Furthermore, at the time, doctors, including his consultant son, had only given him a 25% chance of survival.
