Biography

Willa Cather was born in Virginia on December 7, 1873. An American author known for her novels depicting frontier life on the Great Plains, she wrote such classics as O Pioneers!, My Antonia, and The Song of the Lark. Her literary work contains autobiographical elements and is notable for its air of nostalgia. She was an acquaintance of F. Scott Fitzgerald and exchanged letters with him. She graduated from the University of Nebraska. She then worked in Pittsburgh for the Home Monthly journal.
