John Daly: Golf's Infamous Beer-Drinking Legend

By Oliver Wiener June 17, 2024 05:16

Photo by Alex Slitz/Getty Images

John Daly, a unique player described as much for his prodigious length, aggressive play, and colorful personality as for his 21 professional wins, made an indelible imprint on pro golf.

Daly's story is one of triumph, controversy, and resilience, from his humble beginnings to his ultimate ascent, from his off-course exploits to his enduring legacy.

Early Life and Introduction to Golf 

John Patrick Daly was born on April 28, 1966, and was first introduced to golf at age four.

Emerging from a tumultuous upbringing, Daly's raw gift for the game shone through as he burst onto the professional golfing stage.

His uncouth manner and un-country club-like appearance were a departure from the norm of professional golf.

Rise to Fame on the PGA Tour

Daly burst onto the scene in 1991 with a zero-to-hero win at the PGA Championship, where he was the ninth and final alternate.

The shock victory helped him to the esteemed PGA Tour Rookie of the Year accolade and paved the way for his unorthodox career.

And in 1995, Daly secured his place in golf history by winning The Open Championship in a playoff, reinforcing his extraordinary skill and captivating fans worldwide with his charismatic personality.

100 beers a Night and Partying During Tournaments

By far, the most publicized part of Daly's career was his reputation for drinking beer and partying during tournaments.

Nicknamed "Wild Thing," he was known for his eccentric conduct, odd looks, and devil-may-care mentality.

Daly's policies, such as drinking beer and smoking on the course, were at odds with the more dignified golfers of his era, making him a controversial figure in the golf world.

Golf And Public Image

Daly's unorthodox habits and tendencies wreaked havoc on both his golf game and his public perception.

His "colorful" character quickly led to widespread public perception of The Walrus as a cartoonish figure on Tour. His popularity also had its limits, turning off portions of the golfing public and drawing negative attention to the overall image of professional golf.

Given all the controversies surrounding his bad behavior, Daly's consistency and addictive performances on the course meant that he continued to have a rock-solid place in the golfing world.

Behind the Scenes

Off the course, Daly has struggled with alcoholism and personal hardship. But he has overcome these obstacles with resilience and a rare genuine connection to fans.

His role in numerous charitable causes and his often rabelaisian off-course behavior only serves to deepen his enigmatic presence and provide a more down-to-earth side to his towering personality.

The Wild Thing, John Daly

This guy burst onto the scene with an entirely new prototype for a modern-day golfer and has since proved to have two-time major champion status by never apologizing for anything.

Forever remembered as golf's rowdy beer-drinking legend, his larger-than-life personality and beer-loving, bad-boy rep will never forsake his name in the sport of golf.

No matter the controversies or on- and off-course struggles, Daly was an extraordinarily colorful, fan-friendly individual who has been truly unique and unforgettable in golf history.
