Merle Colby Allin, Jr. (born September 1, 1953) is an American electric bass guitarist who is best known for his work with the band The Allin Brothers. He is the elder brother of the late punk rock singer/songwriter/performance artist GG Allin, who was also a musician. Malpractice, The AIDS Brigade, and The Murder Junkies were the three primary ensembles that included GG Allin, and Allin played bass for all of them. With the help of original drummer Donald (“Dino Sex”) Sachs and a rotating cast of younger players, he is presently leading the Murder Junkies, his brother’s last backup band. Allin was also a member of the Boston punk band Thrills (also known as City Thrills) in the late 1970s, which recorded numerous singles and opened for performers such as David Johansen, the Cars, and the Ramones, among others. In the following year, he joined the rock band Cheater Slicks, where he contributed to their first album, “On Your Knees,” on bass guitar. Allin’s hallmark is an enormous toothbrush moustache that he wears every day.
