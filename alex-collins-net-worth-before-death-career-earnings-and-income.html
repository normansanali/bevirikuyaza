Alex Collins Career & Death

On November 4, 2020, Collins inked a deal with the Seattle Seahawks, joining their practice squad.

His active roster participation saw notable elevations on November 7, 2020, November 14, 2020, and January 2, 2021, aligning with the team’s matchups against the Buffalo Bills, Los Angeles Rams, and San Francisco 49ers during weeks 9, 10, and 17, respectively.

Following each game, he reverted to the practice squad.

He etched his mark with a touchdown against the Los Angeles Rams, and in the game against the San Francisco 49ers, his contribution included a game-clinching eight-yard touchdown run, gaining 29 yards on five carries with less than two minutes remaining.

A repeat elevation took place for the Wild Card Round game against the Rams, followed by his return to the practice squad post-game.

As the season concluded on January 18, 2021, his practice squad contract with the team expired.

The journey continued as Collins renewed his association with the team on February 24, 2021.

In the subsequent 2021 season, his performance featured 108 carries that amassed 411 rushing yards, including two touchdowns, and complemented by nine receptions for 87 receiving yards. He also earned the starting position in six games.

Transitioning into 2023, on January 14, Collins embarked on a new chapter by signing with the Memphis Showboats of the United States Football League (USFL).

During a game against the Houston Gamblers on April 29, 2023, he showcased versatility by throwing a touchdown pass.

Unfortunately, his active participation was hampered, leading to his placement on the team’s injured reserve list on May 4.

Tragedy struck on the evening of August 13, 2023, as Collins lost his life in a fatal traffic collision in Lauderdale Lakes, Florida.

The motorcycle he was operating collided with an SUV, resulting in his passing.

At the tender age of 28, Collins was declared deceased at the scene of the accident.
