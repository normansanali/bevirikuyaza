NBA YoungBoy Releases &quot;Richest Opp&quot; Intro Song, &quot;B*tch Let's Do It&quot;

Yesterday, NBA Youngboy shared the tracklist for his upcoming mixtape, Richest Opp, on Twitter. The mixtape will consist of 17 tracks, which is a lot considering he released a 33-song album in April. However, there is a good reason for his frequent releases, as he has been on house arrest for years due to legal issues stemming from federal gun charges. Despite his house arrest, NBA Youngboy continues to achieve impressive career milestones. He recently became the youngest rapper ever to have 100 songs on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, joining the ranks of elite rappers like Nicki Minaj, Drake, Lil Wayne, and Jay-Z.

Despite his ongoing issues with Lil Durk and DJ Akademiks, the rapper is seemingly living a relatively peaceful life in Utah. However, it seems pretty clear that even living in the middle of nowhere doesn't mean you escape celebrity feuds. Earlier, the rapper released the lyrics to a pretty scathing diss track called "F*ck The Industry Pt. 2." In the post, he highlighted disses of huge names like Drake, J. Cole, and Lil Yachty. Now, ahead of his new release, YoungBoy has dropped the intro track and a music video.

YoungBoy Struts Around His House In New Video

The opening track of NBA YoungBoy's mixtape Richest Opp is called "B*tch Let's Do It." In the song, YoungBoy raps over an aggressive beat and takes aim at Lil Durk and DJ Akademiks through several subliminal shots. The Louisiana hip-hop-influenced track sets the tone for the rest of the mixtape as YoungBoy talks about his drug and firearm dependency, as well as his connection to several murders.

The video for the track features NBA YoungBoy strutting around his beautiful Utah home. The rapper focuses solely on the track, not providing much in the way of flashy visuals. As he is still on house arrest, filming any type of over-the-top visual is likely pretty difficult. YoungBoy manages to make do with what he has access to, however.
