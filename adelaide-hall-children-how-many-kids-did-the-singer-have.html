Jazz vocalist and performer Adelaide Louise Hall (1901–1993) was American-born but lived in the UK. She was a key player in the Harlem Renaissance and is regarded as one of the first jazz singers in history.

She had an eight-decade-long recording career and was the first female vocalist to perform and record with Duke Ellington.

Adelaide Hall Profile Explored

Hall started her career in the chorus line of Broadway musicals after being born in Brooklyn, New York. She joined Duke Ellington’s band in 1927, and the two of them collaborated on the iconic song “Creole Love Call.”

Additionally, she made appearances in a number of films, such as “Stormy Weather” (1943) and “A Star Is Born” (1954).
