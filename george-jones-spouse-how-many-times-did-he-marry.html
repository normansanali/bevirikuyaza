Who was Goerge Jones?

George Glenn Jones was an American musician, singer, and songwriter who is popular for his best-known song “He Stopped Loving Her Today.” George Jones was born on 12 September 1931 in Saratoga, Texas in the US.

George Jones was raised in Colmesneil, Texas, with his brother and five sisters. His father, George Washington Jones, worked at a shipyard and played harmonica and guitar on Sundays, while his mother, Clara, played the piano in the pentecostal church.

Jones first heard country music when he was seven and was given a guitar at the age of nine.

George Jones has been called “The Rolls Royce Of Country Music” and had more than 160 chart singles to his name from 1955 until his death in 2013.

George Jones death

Jones was scheduled to perform his final concert at the Bridgestone Arena on November 22, 2013.

However, on April 18, 2013, Jones was taken to VUMC for a slight fever and irregular blood pressure. His concerts in Alabama and Salem were postponed as a result.

Following six days in intensive care at VUMC, Jones died on April 26, 2013, at age 81.

Former First Lady Laura Bush was among those eulogizing Jones at his funeral on May 2, 2013.

Jones was buried in Woodlawn Cemetery in Nashville.
